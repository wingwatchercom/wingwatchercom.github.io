Links to Wildlife Rehabbers

Within the past two years that we have been a website, the question has arisen more than a few
times as to where to go to find help for an injured creature. "I found a little bird that has fallen from
it's nest and appears to be hurt. Where can I bring it? How do I get it to a rehab facility without
hurting it more or without getting hurt, myself?" Events like this one have a unique affect on us all.
We immediately become emotionally tied to the event and a certain sense of urgency wraps us up
for the duration of it and for long afterwards. But for those of us who have no idea where to find
help or advice, another thing happens. A certain level of fear may develop because of the lack of
direction and know-how. Well, it's happened to me in the past and this page is plopped down, right
here, so you can find the help you need during these "Human/Wildlife Interactions". The Links
below will guide you and give you the proper AND professional advice that is necessary during
a wildlife crisis. If you see email links below, they aren't really links so copy the address to paper
and type it in your personal email preference. We won't link directly to anyone's email address.
WING WATCHERS.com does not imply to be related to or associated with these links.

We have received permission from the following, to link up. They have committed themselves to
helping wildlife which, in turn, aids in the survival of our greatest resource--OUR WILDLIFE.
