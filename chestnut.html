Chestnut Hill Road
Litchfield, CT


Habitat:  Chestnut Hill Road is an open area of farmland and suburban residential homes. It is private except along the road at the intersection of Chestnut Hill Road and Camp Dutton Road. Birders visiting this site should remember birding etiquette and respect for landowner's rights. Please remember to take out what you brought in. This is a temporary page just to follow the Hawk migration a little. Migration time for Hawks starts in mid-September and should end by mid to later October.
Hawk watchers have been recording the activities of Hawks for 25 years from this site. While I was there, a group of eight were waiting for some activity. I was told that two days prior to my visit, 4 to 5 thousand Broadwing Hawks were recorded. To find this site, take Route 63 from the center of Litchfield and go about 1 mile south where you'll find Camp Dutton Road on the left. Follow Camp Dutton Road to the end and you'll be at the intersection of Camp Dutton and Chestnut Hill Rd.
