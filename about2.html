About the Site


  Our website was born in May of 2002 after a vacation to sunny Florida. I bought my first digital camera down there and brought back many bird photos. My sister, Cathi (deceased now), offered to help me build a website to house my photos. After a learning process, we both designed and formatted the original Wing Watchers.com and things were great. Over the last 4 years the site has evolved into the version that you will visit, now. I have learned to do webmaster chores like adding new content, fresh links, and making my own page backgrounds. I hope you will enjoy and learn something from all the effort and devotion that the Wing Watchers Crew puts into this site.

  Once you click on the "ENTER" button on the front page, you will be wisked away to a page of menus and a very cool environmental newsfeed, updated regularly. Deeper into the site you will be presented with a special page for the kids with many fun things to do and visit, also a large volume of bird, insect, animal, and wild and domestic flower photos. Just follow the simple directions on each photo page and the navigation system is easy. We won't let you get lost!!! All of the bird photos have a clickable name tag that will bring up an inline box with descriptions of that species. This is all possible because of free content allowed by eNature.com.

  After the main menus of photographs, there is a link to more important pages on our site. You will find some great Nature Poetry, all the references that we use, a whole page of links to other related websites, and a slide show with some odd photos (non-nature).
