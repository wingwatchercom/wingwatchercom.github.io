White-throated Sparrow

"Ole Sam Peabody, Ole Sam Peabody". That's the call you may hear when this sparrow is close by. Here, in Connecticut, we see them mostly in the autumn and winter. They are arriving now from their breeding grounds farther north.

The White-throated Sparrow is a fairly large size sparrow at 6 to 7 inches long and a wingspan of 8 and 3/4 to 10 inches wide. When it is down here, it likes to hide and forage in hedgerows, brush piles, brushy wood edges, and pastures. It will come to feeders for mixed seed but mostly it is a ground feeding type of bird. We throw mixed seed right on the ground for them.

The White-throated Sparrow eats weed seeds and smaller fruits from dogwood, cedar, and spicebush. It also eats ants, beetles, flies,and many other insects.
