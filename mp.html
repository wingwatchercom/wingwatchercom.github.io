The Connecticut Audubon Coastal Center at Milford Point
Milford , CT


Habitat:   The Coastal Center is on a choice locale for breeding shorebirds. Being on the Smith-Hubbell Wildlife Refuge and bordered by Long Island Sound to the south along with the 840 acre Wheeler Wildlife Refuge to the west, endangered bird species like the Piping Plover and Least Terns can find protected nesting sites. On the Centers north side is a huge exspanse of tidal salt marsh and has fresh water where the Housatonic River empties. The building, as you enter, is the office of the environmental management people who do a great job of helping to keep nesters safe and the grounds immaculate. There is a beautiful museum inside and a three story observation tower atop the building. This birding site ranks high on my list not only because of the diverse eco-systems but because it's a real working sanctuary. Enjoy.
