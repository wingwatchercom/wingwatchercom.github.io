Halloween Pennant Dragonfly


   The photo, above, is a dragonfly called the Halloween Pennant. It is called this because of its black, brown, and orange colors. The scientific name given to this helpful insect is "Celithemis eponina". I say they are helpful because they eat mosquitos, midges, and smaller bugs and it keeps the bug population down. Their normal habitat is ponds, lakes, and marshes. But, because they are strong flyers and some get caught up in the winds, they can also be found cruising in a field or meadow and foraging for their food there.

   The Halloween Pennant is approximately 1.5 inches long and has a wingspan of 2.75 to 3.25 inches. When immature, they have a yellowish coloring in the wings and yellow markings on the body. As they get older, the wings turn darker brown-orange or reddish and the yellow body markings disappear. This showy dragonfly is from the family called Libellulidae or Skimmers that make up about 1,000 seperate species throughout the world and about 100 of these species live in the U.S.

References for this article were: "Dragonflies through Binoculars-A Field Guide to Dragonflies of North American" by Sidney W. Dunkle. Also used was the "Roger Tory Peterson's Field Guide for Insects" by Donald J. Borror and Richard E. White.

This photo was taken by Ron Pelletier, digiscoping with a Nikon 4300 and a Swarovski HD80 spotter scope.
