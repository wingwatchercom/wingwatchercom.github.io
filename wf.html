FOUNDATION HEADQUARTERS AREA

The White Memorial Foundation is one of Connecticut's best birding sites. The Foundation has many acres of woodlands, marshes, and meadows all throughout the Litchfield and Morris area. The main area is the Foundation's headquarters, a great museum for the family, Ongley Pond (where kid's can fish), marked trails, a butterfly garden and house, open meadows, marshes, conifer and deciduous forests, and an observation blind at the bird feeders. This main area is bordered on one side by the Bantam River and by Bantam Lake on the other. I'll tell you that the habitat is so different everytime you turn around that I enjoy myself every time I visit. To top that all off, the White Foundation has a few houses for rehabilitating hawks and owls. Take the family and come see this place. Please use the map link on the links page to find Whitehall Rd. off Rte.202 in Litchfield.

FIVE POND AREA

    The Five Pond Area is a large area of mixed forest with five ponds in it. Fawn Pond, Heron Pond, Teal Pond, Beaver Pond and Plunge Pool attract many interesting species of birds and insects and plant life. The wooded roads within its borders are easily accessible and are marked with various color codes. This is one of the many areas that the White Memorial Foundation owns and cares for. These few pictures aren't enough to display the beauty here. Please use the maps on our links page to find the intersection of Rte.63 and Rte.61 in Litchfield, Ct. The parking lot, although small, is right here.

*********************************************

LITTLE POND
    Little Pond is located on Whites Woods Rd. in Litchfield, right near the intersection with Bissel Rd.Parking is along roadside. The pond is about a 1/4 mile walk past a huge field. The pond has a neat boardwalk that goes all the way around and crosses the Bantam River feed-in, over a railed bridge. Lots of marsh, lots of water, lots of birds.Great for water fowl until the freeze. Please use Map Link on our links page to find this area.

*************************************************

CEMETERY POND
    Cemetery Pond is about 1 mile up the road from Little Pond, heading back toward the center of Litchfield. The pond is across from a cemetery and roadside parking is available. It looks to be about a 1 acre pond bordered by reeds and cattails. Another great water fowl place until the freeze.Cemetery Pond is also on Whites Woods Rd. Please use Map Link on our links page.

*************************************************

POINT FOLLEY

    Here is the best inland Duck and Goose watching place that I know of. This little peninsula juts out onto Bantam Lake with a looped trail that allows great views of the lake from both shores. A campground in the summer, its diverse habitat allows for a lot of feed for passerines and waterfowl. In the winter, when the Lake freezes, the area where the observation platform is seldom freezes due to the Bantam River feeding into it. Point Folley is owned and cared for by the Foundation. To find it, take North Shore Drive off Route 202, in Litchfield. I get lost easy so please use Map Link on our links page. Ample parking, too.
