BIRDING TIPS-FALL/WINTER

  1. Autumn, or Fall, means that hunting season is here. When you go birding or nature walking, try not to go where it is legal to hunt. Hunting is not allowed on Sundays. Most hunters are responsible but accidents can happen.

  2. Do not enter private land that is "Posted" or has "No Trespassing" signs unless you have permission from the land owner. Respect the land you are visiting. Take out what you bring in, respect land-owners property and rights.


  3. Make sure your equipment is in proper order. Don't be forgetting things. It takes time to go back to the car while your partner gets the rare one.


  4. Wear proper clothing. Summer is gone and the temperatures are dropping or about to. Many birding areas are still muddy or very moist from the wet spring and summer. Keep dry socks, gloves, or whatever you think you need to get warm again, in your vehicle. It doesn't take long to feel uncomfortable when it's cold and wet outside.


  5. This tip is good for all year. Be aware of your surroundings. Black bears and coyotes have returned to the natural areas of Connecticut. Although these animals rarely interact with humans, you should be alert to the possibility of encountering one.


  6. Let someone else know where you will be. If something happens out in the field, who's going to find you?


  7. Please enjoy your trips. Take the kids and have fun!
