Long-horned Grasshopper Molting


When grasshoppers hatch from eggs they enter the "nymph" stage. This is a smaller form of the adult grasshopper but without wings. On its way to becoming an adult the nymph will molt, or shed, its outer cover several times until it finally reaches its winged stage or adult form. Ron took these photos of a Long-horned Grasshopper family member for an hour, catching a part of what really goes on during this event.
